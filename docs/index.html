50/50 Pesto Spaghetti

40 min

A little pasta, a little zoodle

things you need:
  • 4 TBSP extra-virgin olive oil—divided
  • 2 TBSP unsalted butter—melted
  • 3 cloves garlic—minced
  • 3 thyme sprigs
  • To taste—salt
  • To taste—freshly ground black pepper
  • 6 ounces gluten-free spaghetti
  • 2 medium zucchini, ends trimmed and peeled with a julienne peeler
  • 4 ounce boneless salmon fillets
  • 4 cup store-bought basil pesto
  • For garnish: freshly-grated Parmesan
here's how:
  • Preheat oven to 375°. In a shallow baking dish, combine 2 tablespoons oil, butter, tomatoes, garlic and thyme and place in the oven. Roast for 15 minutes. Remove from oven and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  • In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Add zucchini and cook for an additional 30 seconds. Drain pasta and zucchini and set aside.
  • In a skillet over medium-high heat, add remaining 2 tablespoons oil. Season salmaon with salt and pepper. Place each fillet skin-side up and sear until the fillets lift up easily from the skillet, 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and sear for an additional 2 to 3 minutes. Transfer skillet to oven and roast until each fillet has just cooked through, 7 to 8 minutes.
  • To assemble: Scoop pesto into a large mixing bowl and top with pasta and zucchini blend. Toss together until pasta and zucchini are well coated. Divide pasta mixture between two serving bowls and top with roasted tomatoes and a piece of salmon. Finish each dish with Parmesan and serve.

Arugula, Tomatoes, and Peppery Sauce Spaghetti

30 min

Everybody likes arugula, right?

things you need:
  • 4 cups arugula—washed, dried, stems removed
  • 1/2 cup extra-virgin oil
  • 2 cloves garlic—minced
  • 28 ounces (1 can) whole Italian plum tomatoes—put through food mill to remove seeds
  • To taste—salt
  • To taste—hot red pepper flakes
  • 1 pound gluten-free spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup Pecorino Romano—freshly grated, divided
here's how:
  • Place arugula in the bowl of a food processor, add 2 tablespoons of oil, and process until smooth. Set aside.
  • Heat the remaining oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and stir until it just begins to turn golden. Add the tomatoes, season with salt and red pepper flakes, and simmer over low heat, stirring from time to time, until sauce has a medium-thick consistency, 10 to 12 minutes.
  • Meanwhile, cook spaghetti in boiling salted water according to package directions.
  • While the pasta is cooking, stir the pured arugula into the sauce. Taste and adjust the seasoning and turn off the heat.
  • Drain pasta and place in a large warm serving bowl. Add half of the sauce and half of the cheese and mix until pasta and sauce are well combined. Add more sauce if needed. Taste, adjust the seasoning, and serve with a sprinkle of additional cheese.

Asparagus, Tomato, and Chicken Spaghetti

30 min

Asparagus lovers, prepare to freak out over this sundried tomato creamy pasta

things you need:
  • 12 ounces gluten-free spaghetti
  • 1 TBSP extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • To taste—salt
  • To taste—freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic—minced
  • 1 bunch asparagus—stalks trimmed and cut into 2" pieces
  • 1/2 cup sundried tomatoes—roughly chopped
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
here's how:
  • In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook spaghetti until al dente. Drain.
  • Meanwhile, in a large skillet over medium-high heat, heat oil. Add chicken and season with salt and pepper. Cook until golden and no longer pink, 8 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and let rest 10 minutes, then slice.
  • Add garlic to skillet and cook until fragrant, 1 minute. Add asparagus and sundried tomatoes and cook until asparagus is tender, 8 minutes.
  • Pour in broth and heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Add spaghetti and toss until fully coated.
  • Eat it and try not think about the asparagus stench.

Baby Artichokes with Garlic, Capers, and Olives Spaghetti

30 min

Nothing says spaghetti like artichokes and capers

things you need:
  • 3 pounds baby artichokes
  • 1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic—finely minced
  • 1/3 cup capers—rinsed, patted dry, roughly minced
  • 1/3 cup black olives—roughly minced
  • 2 TBSP Italian parsley—minced
  • To taste—salt
  • To taste—freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pound gluten-free spaghetti
here's how:
  • Remove the outside leaves from the artichokes until you reach the pale tender leaves of the heart. Cut off the green tops and the green bottoms at the base and remove any green parts around the artichokes.
  • Drop the artichokes into boiling water and cook, uncovered, over medium heat until the bottom of artichokes can easily be pierced with a knife, 4 to 5 minutes depending on size. Drain and place in a bowl of iced water to cool. Drain again and pat dry with paper towels. Cut artichokes into thin slices and set aside.
  • In a large bowl that can later accommodate the pasta, combine olive oil, garlic, capers, olives, and parsley and season with salt and a generous amount of pepper. Add the artichoke hearts and stir to incorporate.
  • Meanwhile, cook spaghetti in boiling salted water according to package directions.
  • Drain spaghetti and place in the bowl. Quickly mix everything until pasta and sauce are well combined. Taste, adjust the seasoning, and serve.
